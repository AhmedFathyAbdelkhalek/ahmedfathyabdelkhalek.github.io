GUC Teaching Assistant

I am currently a teaching assistant at the German University in Cairo in the Engineering Design and Production Department, aiding in instructing students in various courses relating to production technology and manufacturing.

Duties

  1. Development and instruction of course syllabuses, quizzes, and exams
  2. Promptly grading student work and providing feedback
  3. Assessing and assisting students to optimize their learning experience
  4. Proctoring exams to provide a fair examination experience to students
  5. Collaborating with other teaching assistants to ensure a high-standard learning experience for students
  6. Co-supervision of course and bachelor projects
I instructed the following courses
  • Advanced CNC: Enhances design and production students' GCode programming skills by adding highly advanced CNC programming using canned cycles on the Sinumerik 840D controller.
  • Industrial Automation Introduces mechatronics students to CNC machining, Gcode programming, and robotics.
  • CAD/CAM: Introduces design and production students to CNC machining and GCode programming using canned cycles.
  • Engineering Production Technology: Introduces first-year engineering students to different conventional and CNC manufacturing machines and teaches them how to program desktop CNC machines using HMIOperate.
  • Material Removal: Introduces five basic machining operations: turning, milling, drilling, shaping, and grinding, their tools and clamping tehcniques, as well as process plans for part manufacturing.
  • Electrical Circuits II: Introduces capacitors and inductors in addition to filters, integrators, and differentiators using operation amplifiers (OpAmps)

GUC Open Day

It was my pleasure and honor to present the German University in Cairo in its Open Day 2024 for new applicants. I was representing the Engineering and Material Sciences (EMS) Faculty as well as the Engineering Design and Production Department. I had yet another opportunity to showcase the metrology lab's high-speed delta 3D printer to future GUCians with their respected parents. I helped shed some light on the different departments inside of EMS and what they offer based on the inquirers interests and goals. Being a mechatronics graduate who is now working in the Design and Production department, I felt confident in providing an honest and unbiased view of both departments.

This event allowed me to further enhance my presentation skills while helping guide applicants to follow their goals at the GUC and hopefully become an outstanding batch of future engineers.

Tutorials in the Manufacturing Workshop

During the Engineering Production Technology course, I had the wonderful opportunity to showcase several popular machines and machining operations to first-year engineering students. In a collaborative effort with the university's workshop staff, we managed to introduce several operations on a variety of machines, including but not limited to:

  • Sand Casting
  • Die Casting
  • Milling
  • Turning
  • Cylindrical Grinding
  • Sheet Metal Cutting
  • Spot Welding
  • Oxy-fuel Welding and Cutting

German President Inauguration of the German International University

I was honoured to have participated in the official inauguration of the German International University - GIU, representing the Design and Production Department at The German University in Cairo during the visit of German President Mr. Frank-Walter Steinmeier. His Excellency was joined by his German delegation, the Egyptian Minister of Higher Education, Prof. Dr. Mohamed Ashour, GUC and GIU founder, Prof. Ashraf Mansour, The German University in Cairo's president, Prof. Yasser Hegazy, and the German International University - GIU's president, Prof. Slim Abdennadher.

During the Research and Technology Exhibition, I had the opportunity to showcase the metrology lab's high-speed delta 3D printer to the German President and highlighted the growth and importance of this incredible technology. Additionally, we discussed some 3D printing unique capabilities, such as manufacturing jointed and flexible objects in one piece, a feature that no other form of manufacturing can produce, cutting down on assembly and postprocessing costs.

This event spotlighted the power of higher education in Egypt and fortified the strong relationship between Egypt and Germany in furthering technological advancements and enlightening education.
